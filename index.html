About

I was born in Belgrade

Belgrade is colorful in the sun, gray in the rain, incredibly lively and surrounded by the Sava and Danube rivers. The lively interface between Orient and Occident is 2000 years old, has been destroyed 40 times and like a phoenix rose again and again from the ashes. Cultural life can compete with that of Berlin: whether exhibitions, concerts or literary events - something is always happening in the Balkan metropolis. And Belgrade is also Zemun, the city on the Danube, which was once a border town between the Habsburg monarchy and the Ottoman Empire and is now a district of Belgrade.
